Welcome to the world of
Charles Grealy!

I graduated from the University of Houston in May 2020 with a Bachelors of Science in mathematics. My career plan going into college was to become a programmer. In order to achieve programming experience, I set out to minor in computer science.

The first computer science class I took was an introductory course about Python and computer science fundamentals. I quickly learned that computer science was a subject that felt rather natural. In the computer science courses, I felt less inclined to take notes as the material was in a crystal clear language that I could comfortably decipher. The adbundance of material on any given subject is shocking compared to that of mathematics. There's YouTube, books, articles, StackOverflow, open source projects like FreeCodeCamp and TheOdinProject and many, many, other sources. So finding assistance was a few clicks away (Thanks, StackOverflow!). Wow, does programming inject serotonin in my viens!

I'm now applying to various entry level programming positions in hopes of gaining employment. Once I lock down a job, I will pursue a Masters and a PhD in computer science.

FAQ: Why didn't you just major in computer science? I chose mathematics over computer science as math was my strongest subject throughout primary school and the focus on logic would strengthen my programming abilities.
